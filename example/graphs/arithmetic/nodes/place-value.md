# Introduction

Every two-digit number is made of tens and ones. Knowing which digit is
which is the foundation for column addition and subtraction.

## KP 1: Naming Tens and Ones

### Worked Example

In 47, the 4 sits in the tens place and the 7 sits in the ones place, so
47 = 4 tens + 7 ones = 40 + 7.

### Problems

1. Break 62 into tens and ones.
2. Break 85 into tens and ones.
3. Which digit of 39 is in the tens place?

## KP 2: Composing Numbers From Tens and Ones

### Worked Example

3 tens + 8 ones makes 30 + 8 = 38.

### Problems

1. What number is 5 tens + 2 ones?
2. What number is 9 tens + 0 ones?
3. What number is 1 ten + 16 ones? (Regroup first.)
